Creating your first video Setup

Lighting and Monitoring your Camera

This is a tutorial for being able to record a self tape, useful for video auditions, interviews or conferences.

Step One - Lighting:

  1. Identify light sources present in the room, and dim/ turn them off
  2. grab your artificial lights (LEDs) and place:
    • one 20-25 degrees in front of the subject (your front light)
    • one 20 - 25 degrees behind your subject (your back light)
    • one pointing at a white surface to add atmospheric light
  3. TIP: add a small colour source at 90 degrees to add some fun to your shot

Step Two - Camera:

  1. After lighting, switch on your camera
  2. If your subject is too bright:
    • turn up the ISO
  3. If your frame is flickering (black lines streak across the image):
    • Lower your shutter speed (NB, do not lower further than 1/50, due to the fact that cameras can record lower than that)
    • TIP: keep your aperture all the way open for a dreamy look with a clear focus on the foreground and a blurred out background

Step Three - Shooting and Editing:

  1. Find a comfortable outfit/ look and shoot away
  2. If you are filming with audio:
    • clap/ slate before each take to easily sync them in post
  3. Import your footage into an editing software such as DaVinci:
    • choose your shots in the cut tab
    • edit in the edit tab
    • colour grade in the colour tab and audio edit in Fairlight
  4. Export your video accordingly, and get ready to share your smile!
